Illustration Direction

All illustration should reference the mascot's visual DNA: bold outlines, flat colors, high energy, cartoon-exaggerated proportions. The mascot is the brand character — any supporting illustrations should feel like they exist in the same universe.

For photography: prefer action shots with natural ice rink lighting. Dark, atmospheric, motion-blurred imagery works well. Avoid overly posed or studio-lit photography for brand hero moments.

10 · DO'S & DON'TS

Brand consistency rules. Follow these to maintain the Cones Belgrade identity across all channels.

✓ Do
✓Use Bebas Neue for all display headlines and hero moments
✓Keep backgrounds dark — the brand lives on dark surfaces
✓Use #00ADF1 blue for primary CTAs and interactive elements
✓Use uppercase + letter-spacing for all UI labels and nav
✓Pair blue and orange — they are equally important, balance them
✓Allow enough white space — bold type does the heavy lifting
✓Use the mascot as the primary hero brand element
✓Apply the floating drop shadow to the mascot on dark backgrounds
✕ Don't
✕Never use the brand on light/white backgrounds without explicit approval
✕Don't use gradients on text — flat colors only
✕Don't mix blue and orange on the same text element
✕Don't use fonts outside the approved stack (no Comic Sans, no Roboto, no system fonts)
✕Never stretch or distort the logo/mascot
✕Don't use more than 2 accent colors per component
✕Don't use emojis in official print/formal communications
✕Never use low-contrast text below WCAG AA 4.5:1 ratio on body copy
Brand Voice

SPEAK LIKE A CONE

Tone

Bold, energetic, community-first. Not corporate. Not arrogant. We're serious about the game but warm about the people.

Do Say

"See you on the ice." "Hit harder. Skate faster. Be a Cone." "The Cone Zone welcomes everyone."

Don't Say

Corporate jargon. Passive voice. Anything that sounds like a press release rather than a teammate talking.
